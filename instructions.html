How to play

Undercover · 5–9 players · 15–30 min

🎭 The roles

Civilian
The majority

You receive the real secret word. Describe it without giving it away — but convincingly enough that others trust you.

Undercover
The spy

You receive a similar but different word. Your goal is to blend in with the civilians without revealing you have a different word.

Mr. White
The blank slate

You receive no word at all. Listen carefully to others' clues to figure out the civilian word — and try not to get caught.

📋 How a round works

1
Everyone gets their role

Pass the phone around. Each player privately sees their role and word (or no word if they're Mr. White).

2
Give a one-word clue

Going around the table, each player says exactly one word that relates to their secret word. No gestures, no extra hints.

3
Discuss and vote

After everyone has spoken, players debate who seems most suspicious. Then everyone votes — the player with the most votes is eliminated.

4
Mr. White's last chance

If Mr. White is eliminated, they get one shot to guess the civilian word. A correct guess means Mr. White wins instantly.

5
Repeat until someone wins

Keep playing rounds until a win condition is met.

🏆 Winning

  • Civilians win when all Undercover agents and Mr. White have been eliminated.
  • Spies win if they survive until only 3 players remain, or if all civilians are eliminated.
  • Mr. White wins by correctly guessing the civilian word upon elimination.
Pro tip: As a civilian, be descriptive enough to prove you know the word — but vague enough not to hand it to Mr. White. As Undercover, mirror the civilians' energy. As Mr. White, give non-committal clues and piece together the word from what others say.
